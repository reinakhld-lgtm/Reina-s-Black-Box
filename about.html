About Me

My name is Reina. I'm 14 years old, Lebanese, growing up in Abu Dhabi.

Since sixth grade, aerospace has left a spark in me that never went away. It grew into something bigger when I discovered a YouTube channel called Mayday, a documentary series about aviation accidents and their investigations.

I watched one episode and couldn't stop. Not because crashes are frightening, but because every single one had a reason. A preventable reason. An engineering reason.

So I started writing them down in a notebook. What went wrong. How it could have been avoided. What I would have designed differently. I draw the fixes. I think about them when I should probably be thinking about other things.

This website is that notebook, made public. Because I believe these lessons matter, and the more people understand them, the safer our skies become.

Reina, Abu Dhabi, May 2026

My Mission

I want to build the safest aircraft and spacecraft in history. Not just safer than what exists today — but safer than anything that has ever flown. Every analysis on this website is one small step toward understanding what that means.

I'm a student researcher documenting my learning journey. These analyses reflect my current understanding and are intended as thought exercises, not professional assessments.
